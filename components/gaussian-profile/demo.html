Airy Disk Pattern

Interactive visualization of diffraction-limited resolution

About This Component

This component demonstrates the Airy disk, the actual diffraction pattern produced when light from a point source passes through a circular aperture (like a microscope objective). This is the true point spread function (PSF) of an ideal optical system.

I(r) = I₀ [2J₁(x)/x]² where x = (2π/λ) · NA · r

J₁: Bessel function of the first kind
λ: Wavelength of light
NA: Numerical aperture of the objective
r: Radial distance from center

Resolution Criteria

Rayleigh Criterion: Two point sources are just resolvable when the center of one Airy disk falls on the first minimum of the other. Distance = 0.61λ/NA

Abbe Diffraction Limit: The theoretical resolution limit. Distance = λ/(2NA)

Try adjusting NA and wavelength to see how they affect the width of the Airy disk and the calculated resolution limits!
