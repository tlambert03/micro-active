Airy Disk Pattern

Interactive visualization of diffraction-limited resolution

About This Simulation

This component demonstrates the Airy disk, the diffraction pattern produced when light from a point source passes through a circular aperture (like a microscope objective). Two point sources are shown so you can explore how well they can be resolved as a function of NA and wavelength.

$$I(r) = I_0 \left[\frac{2\,J_1(x)}{x}\right]^2 \qquad x = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\,\text{NA}\,r$$
$J_1$
Bessel function of the first kind, order 1
$\lambda$
Wavelength of light
NA
Numerical aperture of the objective
$r$
Radial distance from center

Resolution Criteria

Rayleigh
Two points are just resolvable when the center of one Airy disk falls on the first minimum of the other: $d = 0.61\,\lambda/\text{NA}$.
Abbe
Theoretical diffraction limit: $d = \lambda/(2\,\text{NA})$.
Sparrow
Combined profile becomes flat at the midpoint: $d \approx 0.47\,\lambda/\text{NA}$.

Adjust NA and wavelength to see how they affect the Airy disk width and the resolution limits. Use the criterion buttons to snap the separation to each limit.
